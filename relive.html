Case Study

Relive

An AR-powered mobile concept that makes local history more interactive through location-aware storytelling and immersive on-site experiences.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

Dubstech Protothon 2024

Team

4-person interdisciplinary team

Concept Preview

ReLive
ReLive

AR concept screens showing location-driven storytelling and an on-site mobile experience.

Overview

Relive explores how augmented reality can support learning outside the classroom. The concept overlays historical stories, media, and location-based prompts onto real environments so users can discover history while physically moving through a site.

My Contributions & Learnings

  • Mapped the core user journey.I structured the experience from arrival to recap, which made the concept easier to follow and reduced friction between physical movement and digital interaction.
  • Designed interaction patterns for on-site use.I shaped screen hierarchy, prompts, and checkpoints so users could stay oriented while moving through a real environment.
  • Refined the visual system for clarity.I iterated on layouts and prototype screens to keep historical content readable, immersive, and lightweight enough for mobile viewing.
  • Learned to design for context, not just screens.The project strengthened how I think about accessibility, cognitive load, and attention when users are navigating physical spaces.

Team Contributions

  • Defined the concept around place-based learning.The team framed the product around real locations, which turned static historical information into a more active, memorable experience.
  • Aligned on a guided AR flow.We translated the idea into checkpoints, prompts, and narrative beats that gave the prototype a clear beginning, middle, and end.
  • Balanced immersion with accessibility.The team prioritized readability and ease of navigation so the concept stayed usable even in unfamiliar outdoor settings.

Impact

History felt more immediate

The concept connected stories to real-world locations, making learning feel grounded, interactive, and easier to remember.

Navigation became less demanding

Clear prompts and guided checkpoints reduced uncertainty so users could focus on the experience instead of figuring out what to do next.

Key Experience Flow

Step 01

Arrive on site

Users start the experience at a mapped historical location with clear orientation cues.

Step 02

Reveal AR stories

Location-aware prompts surface media, facts, and story layers tied to the surrounding environment.

Step 03

Move through checkpoints

A guided route keeps momentum high while helping users explore the narrative in manageable moments.

Step 04

Review the narrative

A recap view reinforces key takeaways and helps users retain what they just experienced.
