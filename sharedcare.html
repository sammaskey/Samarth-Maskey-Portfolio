Case Study

SharedCare

A privacy-first caregiver portal that helps nursing home teams share clearer, more predictable resident updates with authorized families in one secure place.

Role

Front-End and Back-End Developer

Focus

Secure family communication and plain-language care updates

Approach

Resident consent, privacy-first permissions, HIPAA-aligned design

Overview

SharedCare addresses the communication gap families often feel when a loved one is in long-term care. Instead of only receiving information during urgent moments, families get clearer day-to-day updates through a secure portal designed to reduce anxiety, prevent miscommunication, and support sustainable workflows for busy care teams.

Team Contributions

  • Defined the MVP around resident profiles, secure messaging, permissions, and plain-language explanations so the scope stayed focused on the highest-value caregiver and family needs.
  • Mapped caregiver and family communication workflows to reduce scattered updates and create a more predictable check-in experience for authorized relatives.
  • Prioritized privacy-first requirements, including resident-authorized access decisions and HIPAA-aligned handling, so trust and safety were built into the product from the start.
  • Reviewed the end-to-end experience together and refined feature flows, which improved clarity for busy care teams and reduced confusion for family members reading updates.

My Contributions & Learnings

  • Built the front-end and back-end foundation for the portal, which connected resident data, messaging, and access control features into one unified experience.
  • Implemented the resident profile structure for appointments, medications, lab results, allergies, and prior documentation, which made important care context easier to review in one place.
  • Developed secure permission logic for resident-authorized visibility, which reinforced privacy expectations and clarified who could access specific information.
  • Translated complex care details into clearer interface language, which improved readability for families and strengthened my ability to design for high-stakes communication.

MVP Features

  1. Resident profile with appointments, medications, lab results, allergies, and prior care documentation.
  2. Secure communication channel between the care team and family or resident.
  3. Resident-authorized access controls for deciding who can see specific information.
  4. Plain-language explanations that make care information easier for families to understand.
