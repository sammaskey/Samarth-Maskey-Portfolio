Case Study

SharedCare

A privacy-first caregiver portal that helps nursing home teams share clearer, more predictable resident updates with authorized families in one secure place.

Role

Front-End and Back-End Developer

Focus

Secure family communication and plain-language care updates

Approach

Resident consent, privacy-first permissions, HIPAA-aligned design

Product Visual

A caregiver dashboard that centralizes daily tasks, resident updates, visits, and communication in one place.

Overview

SharedCare addresses the communication gap families often feel when a loved one is in long-term care. Instead of only receiving information during urgent moments, families get clearer day-to-day updates through a secure portal designed to reduce anxiety, prevent miscommunication, and support sustainable workflows for busy care teams.

My Contributions & Learnings

  • Built the portal foundation across front end and back end.I connected resident data, secure messaging, and permissions into one product flow, which made the experience feel unified instead of fragmented.
  • Structured the resident profile experience.I organized appointments, medications, lab results, allergies, and prior documentation so important care context could be reviewed in one place.
  • Implemented resident-authorized access logic.I translated privacy requirements into clearer visibility rules, which reinforced trust and limited who could view sensitive information.
  • Learned to design for high-stakes clarity.Turning care information into plain-language interface content strengthened how I build products where comprehension and reassurance both matter.

Team Contributions

  • Defined the MVP around the most urgent communication gaps.The team prioritized resident profiles, secure messaging, permissions, and plain-language explanations so the first release stayed focused and credible.
  • Mapped caregiver-to-family update flows.We shaped the experience around predictable check-ins, which reduced reliance on fragmented updates and one-off status calls.
  • Built privacy into product decisions early.The team aligned on resident consent and HIPAA-aware handling from the start, which made trust a core part of the experience rather than a late add-on.
  • Refined the product around clarity under pressure.Reviewing flows together helped simplify communication for busy care teams and made updates easier for families to understand quickly.

Impact

Family communication became more predictable

Instead of only hearing updates during urgent moments, families would have a clearer place to check in on day-to-day well-being.

Trust and privacy became more visible

Resident-authorized access and plain-language explanations made the experience feel safer, clearer, and easier to navigate.

MVP Features

Feature 01

Resident profile

Appointments, medications, lab results, allergies, and prior care documentation stay together in one organized record.

Feature 02

Secure communication
